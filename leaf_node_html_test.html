\r\n Comments\r\n
\r\n\r\n
\r\n

The passenger compartment of the Ignis remained stable in the offset deformable frontal impact test. \xc2\xa0Dummy readings indicated good protection of the knees and femurs of the driver and passenger. \xc2\xa0Suzuki showed that a similar level of protection would be provided to occupants of different sizes and those sat in different positions. \xc2\xa0Compression of the driver dummy\'s chest indicated weak protection of this part of the body and injury parameters for the lower legs revealed marginal protection. \xc2\xa0In the full width rigid barrier test, protection of the chest was rated as marginal for both the driver and rear passenger side dummy, as was the head protection for the rear dummy. In the side barrier impact, the Ignis scored full points with good protection of all critical body regions. \xc2\xa0Even in the more severe side pole test, protection of the chest was adequate and that of other body areas was good. \xc2\xa0Tests on the front seats and head restraints revealed good protection against whiplash injuries in the event of a rear-end collision, as did a geometric assessment of the rear seats. \xc2\xa0The Ignis has low-speed autonomous emergency braking as part of its optional \'Dual Camera Brake Support\' safety pack. \xc2\xa0Tests showed that the system performed well, avoiding all collisions in the low-speed test scenarios against a stationary car.

\r\n Comments\r\n
\r\n\r\n
\r\n

The Ignis has, as standard, a seatbelt reminder system for the front and rear seats. \xc2\xa0As part of its optional safety pack, it also has a highway-speed autonomous emergency braking (AEB) system and a lane departure warning system. \xc2\xa0The AEB system performed well in Euro NCAP\'s tests and the lane departure warning system worked correctly in all test scenarios.
